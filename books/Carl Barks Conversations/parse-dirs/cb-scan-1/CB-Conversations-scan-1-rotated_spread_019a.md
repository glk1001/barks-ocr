# CHRONOLOGY

**1966–68** Writes scripts with rough layouts of one more *Uncle Scrooge* story (June 1966) and two more *Donald Duck* stories (Mar. 1967 and Nov. 1968).

**1968** First large-scale exposure of Barks's identity to comic book readers in the USA occurs when the 1962 interview with Willits (expanded by correspondence between Barks and Don and Maggie Thompson) appears in *Comic Art* #7, along with an expanded version of Barrier's "The Lord of Quackly Hall"; in Italy, Barks's name appears in the introduction to a pocket-sized collection of his classic stories, *Vita e Dollari di Paperon De' Paperoni* (*The Life and Dollars of Scrooge McDuck*) (Aug.), although he is not explicitly credited with authorship of the stories.

**1969** Barrier first visits Carl and Garé in June in San Jacinto; Carl and Garé move to Goleta, California.

**1969–73** Produces scripts and rough page layouts for 24 *Junior Woodchucks* comic book stories (Oct. 1969–Mar. 1973).

**1970** The Swedish comic book fanzine *Thud* #8 publishes an article on *Donald Duck* and Carl Barks; Donald Ault pays his first visit to Carl and Garé (Aug.); publication of *Die Ducks* by Grobian Gans (pseudonym), Verlag Heinz Moos, Munich (does not mention Barks but is the first major theoretical book published on the ducks).

**1971** Receives first Shazam award for "Best Writer, Humorous Division" (12 May); receives Disney Duckster award (26 May); Glenn Bray commissions the first Disney Duck oil painting; Barks requests and receives permission to do Disney oil paintings, royalty-free; *Para Leer al Pato Donald* by Ariel Dorfman and Armand Mattelart is published in Chile; Barks's name is first mentioned in Germany in *Comics: Anatomie eines Massenmediums* by Wolfgang J. Fuchs and Reinhold C. Reitberger, Gräfelfing bei München, Heinz Moos Verlag; the authors misspelled his name as Barx in the first printing.

# CHRONOLOGY

**1971–76** With Disney's permission, Barks creates his first series of approximately 130 oil paintings of the ducks; sells most of the first 75 paintings for $150–$350 on a one-to-one special order basis through a waiting list monitored by Ault until 1973.

**1972** "Carl Barks, Aloha" by Evert Geradts (*Aloha*, 28 Jan. 1972) is the first article on Carl Barks to be published in The Netherlands; on 2 Apr., Markku Kivekas and Jukka Rislakka publish the first article revealing Carl Barks's identity in Finland in the leading newspaper *Helsingin Sanomat* (translated title of the article: "The Authors of Donald Duck Were Hidden under the Name of Walt Disney—Honor Is Due to Mr. Carl Barks"); in Aug., Ault teaches the first university course devoted to Barks as a literary figure (in the Department of English, the University of California, Berkeley); during the 1972–73 term Professor Pio Baldelli directs a seminar on Barks at the University of Florence.

**1973** The painting order list approaches 150 entries; Bruce Hamilton and Russ Cochran begin auctioning some of Barks's Disney paintings to the public, and prices for the paintings rapidly escalate to many thousands of dollars; the Norwegian fanzine *Donaldisten* begins regularly to feature information on Barks; Daan Jippes's "Duckman Barks over Carl Barks," a portrait compiled from interviews and additional information (*Stripschrift* Nov.–Dec.), is the first extended essay on Barks published in The Netherlands.
