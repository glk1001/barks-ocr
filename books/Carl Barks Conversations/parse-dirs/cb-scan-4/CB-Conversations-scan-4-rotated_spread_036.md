# Notes on the Interviewers

**Thomas Andrae** teaches in the cinema department at San Francisco State University. He is cofounder and senior editor of the journal *Discourse*, a former editor of the *Carl Barks Library*, and author of a forthcoming study of Barks's work, *Fables of Desire*.

**Lynda Ault** has a background in comparative literature and has been an award-winning professional photographer as well as a medical photographer. She is currently a realtor in Gainesville, Florida.

In the mid-1960s **J. Michael Barrier** created the magazine *Funnyworld*, in which he published articles on Barks and serially published the carefully researched Carl Barks bibliography, all of which was gathered for his first book, *Carl Barks and the Art of the Comic Book*. In 1999 his *Hollywood Cartoons: American Animation in Its Golden Age* was published by Oxford University Press.

**Bill Blackbeard** is founder of the San Francisco Academy of Comic Art, co-editor of *The Smithsonian Collection of Newspaper Comics*, and compiler and editor of countless collections of comic strip reprint books and scholarly introductions to them. One of the most influential figures in twentieth-century comics scholarship, he is one of the people most responsible for the preservation and collection of late-nineteenth- and early-twentieth-century comics.
