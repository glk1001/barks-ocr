LA: Well, early exposure to those kinds of stories can stick in your consciousness.

CB: For a short time while I was living in Santa Rosa, when I was 10, I read a few of the Horatio Alger books.

LA: Don's father read those.

CB: *The Speedwell Boys on Motorcycles*. There were a bunch of imitators out there.

LA: Did Horatio Alger inspire you to go out and start shining shoes or get a paper route or whatever you could?

CB: That's right. They always made a big success.

LA: Did they affect you? Did they inspire you to work hard and pull yourself up by your bootstraps?

CB: Oh, I don't know. It gave me the feeling that if I worked hard enough and worked well at anything that I might get a reward out of it.

## His Brother's Storytelling

LA: When you were a child did you make up stories?

CB: No, but my brother did.

LA: Did he tell them to you? Were you his audience?

CB: We were just little bits of guys lying in bed at night. He would make up stories.

LA: Was he older or younger?

CB: He was a year and a half older. He would make up these stories, and there was always some despicable character. He would be a guy that wore a white collar and worked in an office, and my brother called him "The Flat Butt Man."

LA: Because he sat in a chair all day?

CB: I think it was a bunch of kids who were his heroes, and whatever they wanted to do, they had to outwit this "Flat Butt Man" to accomplish what they wanted to do, whether it was to take a trip or catch some horses—any sort of adventure like that. Now why he had that talent then, and I didn't have any of it...

LA: What did he do when he grew up? What was his life work?

CB: Oh, he became a farmer and a carpenter.

## Self Education in Writing

DA: Well, I was curious about what your experience of school was like. You were such a good researcher and you had really good study habits. Did you learn to do that in school or did you just teach yourself to do that?

CB: I don't know. I just liked to do it. I liked to understand what I was reading about. And I liked to know who wrote the stuff. What authority they had for writing it. Yeah, I was a little curious as I grew up. I was curious about authors and their backgrounds. In fact, I guess it may be the teachers I had.
