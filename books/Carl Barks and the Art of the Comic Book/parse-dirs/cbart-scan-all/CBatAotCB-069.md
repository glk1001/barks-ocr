But if Barks went overboard in his use of realistic illustration, and veered dangerously  close to sentimentality, it was only because

his work in the early fifties was so much more ambitious — if that's the word — than it had been a few years earlier. He was dealing with a much wider range of characters, emotions, and subjects than he had before, and since he was doing so much more, it wasn't surprising that sometimes, in some areas, he would do too much.
