through variety stores. Barks's work has continued to be reprinted in the Whitman comic books, and it has also been reprinted in a number of hardcover and semi-hardcover books from Abbeville Press since 1978. The Abbeville books published to date (all of them adapted from Italian reprint volumes) have radically altered the composition of Barks's panels and pages and often changed dialogue as well; they are completely inadequate. Western itself has done a little better by Barks, publishing a few of his stories in a series of "Dynabrite Comics" that enjoy somewhat
better paper and printing (and higher prices) than the other Whitman comics. The color, unfortunately, is garish.
In closing, let me encourage everyone who finds errors or omissions in this book—and particularly in the bibliography—to write to me at 226 N. Saint Asaph Street, Alexandria, Va. 22314.
Michael Barrier
