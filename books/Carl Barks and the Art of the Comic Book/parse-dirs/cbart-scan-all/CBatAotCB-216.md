The following is a list of the Disney cartoons on which Barks worked as a story man, according to his own records. The date in parentheses is the cartoon's release date.

In some cases, Barks's role in the work on a story is specified in an outline that was distributed throughout the studio before work on the story was completed; copies of such outlines have been preserved in the Disney Archives. The initials "SD" after a cartoon's release date indicate that, according to an outline, Barks was the story director on the cartoon. The initials "SC" indicate that he was identified as a member of the story crew, technically subordinate to a story director — although as Barks and other former Disney story men have said, such distinctions were not much honored at the time.

Typically, a year or more would elapse between the circulation of an outline and the release of the finished cartoon; for example, the outline for *Sea Scouts* was distributed on March 26, 1938, and the cartoon was released on June 30, 1939.

*Trombone Trouble* had the working title *Superman Duck* at the time Barks was involved with it.

All of the above cartoons were directed by Jack King, with the following exceptions: *Sea Scouts*, Dick Lundy; *Bone Trouble*, Jack Kinney; *Put-Put Troubles*, Riley Thompson; *The Golden Eggs*, Wilfred Jackson; *The Village Smithy*, Dick Lundy; *The Army Mascot*, Clyde Geronimi.

All of the cartoons on the list are Donald Duck cartoons except for *Bone Trouble* and *The Army Mascot*, both of which star Pluto.

Barks's records show that he worked on cartoon stories listed under the titles "Northwest Mounted," "Prospector," "Balloon," "Shooting Gallery," "Beaver Hunt," and "Salesman," all of which were shelved. The Disney Archives has material — sketches, outlines, gag suggestions, or story meeting notes — matching all of these titles except "Salesman," and has an outline with Barks' name on it and sketches for a cartoon that was to have been titled *Donald Munchausen*.
