10. Next, I was asked to combine Benny with Barney Bear into an 8-page, 8-panel format as a regular feature, starting in *Our Gang* No. 11.

Benny is the burro from a 1942 Rudolf Ising cartoon for MGM, *Little Gravel Voice*; he has no name in that cartoon. "About the name of Benny Burro, I don't believe I named him. My guess would be Eleanor Packer, who edited the comic books for Western in those days." (Mar. 8, 1970, letter to Michael Barrier)

## 9 - Jan.-Feb. 1944 - 60 pages

HAPPY HOUND (Detective) - 7 7/8 (pp. 21-28) - A prisoner escapes from Alca-sing Prison on Happy's day off, when Happy wants to go fishing, and Happy keeps crossing paths with the crook despite his best efforts not to. (*July 31, 1943*)

"Happy Hound" is actually Droopy, and the prisoner is the wolf who was Droopy's opponent in many of Tex Avery's MGM cartoons.

A circulation statement fills out the eighth page.

BENNY "THE LONESOME BURRO" - 8 (pp. 41-48) - Benny plays follow-the-leader with a little mountain goat until his sneeze causes a snow slide that carries them into a wolf's cave. (*Aug. 12, 1943*)

## 10 - Mar.-Apr. 1944 - 52 pages

BENNY "THE LONESOME BURRO" - 8 - Benny goes to the desert to see "the most beautiful bird in the world," a macaw who plays vicious tricks on him. (*Oct. 9, 1943*)

## 11 - May-June 1944 - 52 pages

BARNEY BEAR AND BENNY BURRO - 8 - Barney and Benny come to a Western town whose inhabitants are fleeing because of mysterious groans that are coming from a huge "horn" hollowed out of a mountain by a madman. (*Dec. 16, 1943*)

The first story with Barney and Benny as a team. They never appeared together in animated cartoons until *Half-pint Palomino*, a 1953 MGM cartoon directed by Dick Lundy (although Barney did have an unnamed burro as a foil in *The Prospecting Bear*, a 1941 cartoon directed by Rudolf Ising).

HAPPY HOUND Detective - 6 - Happy apathetically captures the wolf prisoner again, then lets him visit his "mother," actually a disguised hoodlum. (*Oct. 30, 1943*)

Barks submitted this as an eight-page story; it was reduced to six by rearranging and altering the panels so that more could be accommodated on a page.
