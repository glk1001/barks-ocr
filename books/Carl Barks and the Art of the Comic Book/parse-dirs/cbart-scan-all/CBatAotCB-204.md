*March 29, 1972* - Oil painting for a Christmas one-shot.

Barks: "The Disney Christmas comic one-shot was canceled. Reason: the company has gone all out for selling comics in bags four for 59 cents, and they no longer want seasonal stuff that will grow stale during long stays on store racks." (Dec. 26, 1972, letter to Michael Barrier)

*Rejected Ideas for Covers*

Barks submitted a number of ideas for front covers, in the form of rough sketches, that were rejected by Western, and some of those sketches still exist. A few were intended for specific issues, and are noted with the entries for those issues; see the entries for Donald Duck No. 117, January 1968, and Uncle Scrooge No. 50, July 1964; No. 54, December 1964, and No. 62, March 1965.

The other known surviving sketches can be described as follows:

As Donald bends over to pick a flower, two of the nephews hold a cape in front of his rump, and the third nephew, wearing bull's horns, charges it.

Donald, dressed as a hunter and holding a moose call, looks perplexed as a tiny mechanical moose walks in front of him; the nephews laugh in the background.

Uncle Scrooge puts a hot brick of twenty-four-carat gold in his bed to warm it.

Donald is carrying an armload of Christmas packages with a "wide load" sign hung on them; the nephews are waving lanterns to clear the way.

In a cover for *Uncle Scrooge* that illustrates a story that was apparently never published — at least not under the title on the drawing, "Lost in Davy Jones' Locker" — the ducks exult over treasure chests and gold bullion; the mast of a sunken ship is visible in water behind them.
