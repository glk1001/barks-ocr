# OTHER COMICS MATERIAL

## BETTER LITTLE BOOKS AND NEW BETTER LITTLE BOOKS

Barks did no original work for the Better Little Books; his comic-book work was reprinted in them. In some books (identi- fied as "All Picture Comics"), his comic-book panels were reprinted one to a page—trimmed as necessary—and some panels were deleted. In other books,the dialogue was deleted from the reprinted panels and the panels were printed on pages facing a page of text, in what is considered the standard Better Little Book format. Better Little Books, successors to the Big Little Books of the 193Os, were approximately four and a half inches square and three and a half inches thick, although their thickness varied over the years. The New Better Little Books were approximately five and a half inches tall and three and one eighth inches wide with fewer pages than their predecessors. Their format also differed, in that the comics panels and text were printed together on each page, instead ofon facing pages.In the following list, the title, number and date ofthe Better orNew Better Little Book is followed by information about the Barks reprints that each book contains.

*Donald Duck and Ghost Morgan's Treasure* - Better Little Book 1411 (1946) - All Pictures Comics - Reprinted from "DONALD DUCK Finds Pirate's Gold" in Donald Duck Four Color No. 9 (1942).

## OTHER CHILDREN'S BOOKS

*Donald Duck and the Boys* - Whitman book, No. 845 (1948) - A book similar to the Better Little Books, in that each page of text faces an illustration, but with these differences: The pages are slightly larger (five and one-quarter inches wide and five and five-eighths inches tall), the book has only ninety-six pages (plus covers) and red ink is spotted on many of the pages. The story is a retelling in text form (written by someone other than Barks) of the "DONALD DUCK" story by Barks in Walt Disney's Comics No. 74, November 1946, with forty-eight new illustrations by Barks, similar to (but not duplicating) his original comic book panels. *(Dec. 11, 1947)*

*Uncle Scrooge the Lemonade King* - Top Top Tales No. 2465 (1960) - 28 pages, plus covers - Barks did the pencil roughs for this story, which consists of very little text and many colored illustrations; the coloring was added by Norman McGary. In addition to the twenty-eight pages of the story itself, Barks did the roughs for the front cover and for the illustration used for the inside front and back covers. *(Oct. 17, 1959)*
