*Donald Duck and the Christmas Carol* - Little Golden Book D84 (1960) - 24 pages, plus covers - Barks did the pencil roughs for the interior pages only; color was then applied by another artist. At least two slightly different editions of this book exist: (1) The original edition, whose price was twenty-five cents, includes a Christmas tree in the background of the front cover; the "Little Golden Book" line is at the top of the cover. (2) The second edition has no Christmas tree in the background, and the "Little Golden Book" line has been moved to the bottom of the front cover; the price is twenty-nine cents. The back covers of the two editions contain different advertising matter for other Golden Books, and the inside covers also differ in various respects. (Nov. 28, 1958)

A December 15, 1958, letter to Barks from Donald T. McLaughlin of Western Publishing asks him to make some minor changes in the pencil roughs for this book, and mentions making Scrooge larger on the cover; however, Scrooge is not on the cover of the published book, and Barks says the cover drawing is not his. In the 1974 interview, he could not recall

doing a cover drawing with Scrooge in it.

Barks said in the 1974 interview that he had anticipated that his work on the two children's books "would be pleasant, and a nice break," but that he found it too exacting, because the pencil drawings had to be precise guides for the color artist.

*Donald Duck* - Whitman Comic Book No. 8 (1962) - 140 pages plus covers - This book reprints (in black and white, except for blue spotted on the title pages of stories) most of the contents of Walt Disney's Comics Nos. 209-213, February 1958 through June 1958, including the "DONALD DUCK" stories by Barks in each issue.
