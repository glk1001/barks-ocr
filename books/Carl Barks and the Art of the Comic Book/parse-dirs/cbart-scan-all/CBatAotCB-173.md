Barks panels are reproduced with each article. (The dialogue in the *Popular Science* panels is not by Barks; it evidently was re-translated from the Danish.)

**105 (9/9) - June 1949 - 52 pages**

DONALD DUCK - 10 - Donald goes to Thrushwhistle Glen to practice echoes, and the nephews are there to supply them — for ten cents each. *(Jan. 13, 1949)*

**106 (9/10) - July 1949 - 52 pages**

DONALD DUCK - 10 - The nephews return from summer camp with a collection of pets who rout both Donald and the Black Mask Burglar. *(Jan. 27, 1949)*

**107 (9/11) - August 1949 - 52 pages**

DONALD DUCK - 10 - Donald accidentally drinks "liquid isotopes" that give him the incredible strength of Super Snooper, a comic-book superhero. *(Mar. 22, 1949)*

Barks: "My notes say I paid Dana Coty $25 for the idea."

**108 (9/12) - September 1949 - 52 pages**

Front cover (art only): Donald and the nephews sail on a small boat. *(Feb. 23, 1949)*

DONALD DUCK - 10 - Instead of eating an enormous frog named B-36 as he had originally planned, Donald enters the frog in a jumping contest. *(Apr. 14, 1949)*

**109 (10/1) - October 1949 - 52 pages**

Front cover (art only): Donald is hit by a newspaper flung from the nephews' bicycle handlebar slingshot. *(June 16, 1949)*

DONALD DUCK - 10 - The nephews buy a water divining stick from an old peddler and try to convince Donald that it really works. *(Apr. 28, 1949)*

**110 (10/2) - November 1949 - 52 pages**

DONALD DUCK - 10 - Donald comes to fetch his nephews home from Camp Waha-Go-Gaga (where Gladstone is the camp guard) and tangles with three porridge-hungry bears. *(May 12, 1949)*

**111 (10/3) - December 1949 - 52 pages**

DONALD DUCK - 10 - Daisy breaks up with Donald and returns a bundle of steamy love letters, which the nephews later take to Gladstone's house in a box of borrowed books. *(Aug. 4, 1949)*

**112 (10/4) - January 1950 - 52 pages**

DONALD DUCK - 10 - The nephews trick Donald into thinking that he has been bitten by a strange insect and has slept forty years. *(Aug. 24, 1949)*

**114 (10/6) - March 1950 - 52 pages**

DONALD DUCK - 10 - Donald, who has bragged of his skiing exploits, is pursued by foreign spies as he carries serum to a stricken community through a record snowfall. *(Oct. 13, 1949)*

**117 (10/9) - June 1950 - 52 pages**

DONALD DUCK - 10 - Donald and Gladstone compete to find the first daisy and so be Daisy's partner at the Wildflower Club's picnic lunch. *(Oct. 27, 1949)*

Reprinted: *Walt Disney's Comics* No. 299, August 1965.

**124 (11/4) - January 1951 - 52 pages**

DONALD DUCK - 10 - Uncle Scrooge becomes allergic to money and must let Donald manage his fortune. *(Aug. 10, 1950)*

**125 (11/5) - February 1951 - 52 pages**

DONALD DUCK - 10 - The nephews take their St. Bernard dog into the snow to pass the test that will make them brigadier generals in the Junior Woodchucks. *(Aug. 31, 1950)*

First appearance of the Junior Woodchucks.

Reprinted: *Huey, Dewey and Louie Junior Woodchucks* No. 5, April 1970.

**126 (11/6) - March 1951 - 52 pages**

DONALD DUCK - 10 - Uncle Scrooge's fortune, which he has hidden in a huge corn crib on a farm, is picked up by a tornado and scattered all over the country, so that everyone becomes a millionaire. *(Sept. 14, 1950)*

Barks has said that he has always considered this story "technically well done. It had a rhythm that could almost have been set to music." *(Oct. 14, 1969, letter to Winston Ljungdahl)*
Reprinted: *Walt Disney's Comics* No. 363, December 1970; Les Daniels, *Comix: A History of Comic Books in America*, New York: Outerbridge & Dienstfrey, 1971 (see Appendix D).

**127 (11/7) - April 1951 - 52 pages**

DONALD DUCK - 10 - The nephews try all day to play an April Fool's trick on Donald, but all their efforts backfire. *(Sept. 28, 1950)*

Reprinted: *Walt Disney's Comics* No. 344, May 1969.

**128 (11/8) - May 1951 - 52 pages**

DONALD DUCK - 10 - Donald and Gladstone are rivals for the lead in a play about knights and dragons, to be staged by Daisy's drama club. *(Nov. 30, 1950)*

**129 (11/9) - June 1951 - 52 pages**

DONALD DUCK - 10 - Donald tears out his garden and replaces it with a swimming pool that is taken over by a horde of obnoxious children. *(Dec. 7, 1950)*

Reprinted: *Walt Disney's Comics* No. 335, August 1968.

**130 (11/10) - July 1951 - 52 pages**

Front cover (art only): The nephews provide power with eggbeaters for Donald's boat. *(Feb. 22, 1951)*

DONALD DUCK - 10 - Donald discovers that the old dime he has given Uncle Scrooge to pay a debt is actually worth five dollars and he tries to get it back. *(Dec. 28, 1950)*

Reprinted: *Uncle Scrooge and Money*, 1967.
