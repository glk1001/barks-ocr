# GOLDEN DIGEST (Gold Key)
All issues of *Golden Digest* were approximately 4 7/8 inches wide and 6 5/8 inches tall.
## 18 - July 1971 - 164 pages
BARNEY BEAR AND BENNY BURRO Camping Out - 8 (pp. 66-73) - A reprint of "BARNEY BEAR AND BENNY BURRO" in *Our Gang* No. 24, July 1946.
Changes: Title changed as indicated.
## 22 - March 1972 - 164 pages
DROOPY The Hero Hound - 11 (originally 8) (pp. 143-153) - A reprint of "DROOPY" in *Tom and Jerry Winter Carnival* No. 2, 1953.
Changes: Title changed as indicated. The panels for the original eight-page story have been trimmed and partially re-drawn so that the pages are three panels deep.
## 41 - January 1975 - 132 pages
DROOPY King of the Hounds - 11 (originally 8) (pp. 65-75) - A reprint of "DROOPY" in *Tom and Jerry Winter Carnival* No. 1, 1952.
Changes: Title changed as indicated. The panels for the original eight-page story have been trimmed and partially re-drawn so that the pages are three panels deep.
# GRANDMA DUCK'S FARM FRIENDS (Dell)
## Four Color 1010 - July-Sept. 1959 - 36 pages
GRANDMA DUCK'S FARM FRIENDS and the Flying Farm Hand - 8 - art only - Dumbo comes to Grandma's farm, at the nephews' invitation, but scares the animals until the nephews hit on using him as a scarecrow. *(Mar. 6, 1959)*
GRANDMA DUCK'S FARM FRIENDS and A Honey of a Hen - 7 - art only - Scrooge, at Grandma's farm to buy some produce, fakes an accident so that he can enjoy Grandma's cooking for a few days, but he gives himself away when he sees a hen in danger from a chicken hawk. *(Mar. 6, 1959)*
GRANDMA DUCK'S FARM FRIENDS and the Weather Watchers - 7 - art only - Gyro invents a mechanical weather-vane for Grandma's farm, but Grandma's goat interferes with it, causing it to give out alarming forecasts. *(Mar. 6, 1959)*

Reprinted: *Walt Disney comics Digest* No. 56, December 1975.
GRANDMA DUCK'S FARM FRIENDS and the Sheepish Cowboys - 6 7/8 - art only - The nephews ride on sheep when their horse runs away, but the other sheep follow their mounts. The nephews pen up the sheep in a gully, where the Big Bad Wolf finds them. *(Mar. 6, 1959)*
The Dell Pledge fills out the last page.
Reprinted: *Walt Disney Comics Digest* No. 50, December 1974; *Walt Disney's Comics* No. 293, February 1965.
## Four Color 1073 - Jan.-Mar. 1960 - 36 pages
Front cover: Grandma cracks an egg for Gus Goose's breakfast and finds a chick inside. *(July 18, 1959)*
GRANDMA DUCK Mopping Up - 1 (inside front cover) - art only - Grandma puts down fly paper to keep Gus from tracking up her kitchen floor. *(July 28, 1959)*
GRANDMA DUCK'S FARM FRIENDS Touche' Toupee' - 6 - art only - After Grandma's horse eats some of his hay and apples, her neighbor Si Bumpkin accuses her of stealing his eggs. *(July 15, 1959)*
Reprinted: *Walt Disney Comics Digest* No. 39, [February] 1973.
GRANDMA DUCK'S FARM FRIENDS The Snow Chaser - 6 - art only - Gyro uses a "snow chaser" to vaporize snow on Grandma's farm, but through a series of misadventures, the vaporized snow builds up in storm clouds overhead. *(July 28, 1959)*
GRANDMA DUCK'S FARM FRIENDS Free Ski Spree - 6 - art only - Scrooge tries winter sports at Grandma's, but has little luck at ice skating, tobogganing or skiing. *(July 15, 1959)*
Reprinted: *Walt Disney Comics Digest* No. 20, February 1970.
## Four Color 1161 - Feb.-Apr. 1961 [(January) 1961] - 36 pages
GRANDMA DUCK'S FARM FRIENDS The Whole Herd of Help - 8 - art only - Scrooge gives Grandma an elephant that he has received as a gift, and the elephant proves useful on the farm. *(Aug. 8, 1960)*
Reprinted: *Walt Disney Comics Digest* No. 34, April 1972.
GRANDMA DUCK'S FARM FRIENDS The Day the Farm Stood Still - 6 - Grandma wakes to find the farm silent, waiting for the return of a precocious chick who has upset all of the surrounding farmers with his efforts to help them. *(Aug. 8, 1960)*
Reprinted: *Walt Disney Comics Digest* No. 10, April 1969.
GRANDMA DUCK'S FARM FRIENDS The Training Farm Fuss - 7 - art only - Gladstone practices being a matador, using one of Grandma's bulls, then heads for Mexico. *(Aug. 8, 1960)*
Reprinted: *Walt Disney Comics Digest* No. 21, April 1970.
GRANDMA DUCK'S FARM FRIENDS The Reversed Rescue - 7 - art only - Donald flies in a helicopter to Grandma's snowbound farm, but is shot down by the Beagle Boys, and Grandma and the nephews have to rescue him. *(Aug. 8, 1960)*
Reprinted: *Walt Disney Comics Digest* No. 19, January 1970.
