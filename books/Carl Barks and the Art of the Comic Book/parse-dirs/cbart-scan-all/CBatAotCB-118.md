**No number - (December) 1945 - 20 pages**

DONALD DUCK's Best Christmas - 8 - Donald rents a horse and sleigh so that he and the nephews can take all their Christmas presents and food to Grandma's house. A storm comes up and they take refuge with a poor widow and her two children. *(Aug. 31, 1945)*

**No number - 1946 - 20 pages**

DONALD DUCK in Santa's Stormy Visit - 8 - Donald and the nephews are tending a lighthouse on Christmas Eve, and Donald is planning to radio ashore for presents. However, an albatross who has taken refuge in the lighthouse wrecks the radio set. *(Mar. 8, 1946)*

**No number - (December) 1947 - 20 pages**

DONALD DUCK in "Three Good Little Ducks" - 8 - The nephews, fearful that they've been too bad all year to get

Christmas presents, try to make it up to Donald on Christmas Eve, but all of their efforts at good deeds backfire. *(Feb. 28, 1947)*

**No number - (December) 1948 - 20 pages**

DONALD DUCK in "Toyland" - 8 - art and rewriting of script - The ducks are invited by Santa Claus to come to the North Pole and test his toys. Donald takes his lumps when the testing begins. *(July 8, 1948)*

Barks's list notes that he was paid twenty dollars for rewriting the script for this story.

**No number - (December) 1949 - 20 pages**

DONALD DUCK in "New Toys" - 8 - The nephews' Christmas list includes a new tricycle, scooter and coaster wagon, but they already have one of each, and Donald tells them not to expect new toys until their old ones are worn out. *(July 7, 1949)*
