Scrooge has a receipt for the debt, and carries it with him to Alaska in a flight bag for the trail of Soapy's lawsuit. # 62 - March 1966 - 36 pages

Front cover: The ducks flee from a ferocious wild girl and her wild dog. (Illustrating "The Queen of the Wild Dog Pack.") *(July 22, 1965)*

Two rejected Barks sketches for the cover of this issue exist. One shows the wild girl lifting a car in which the ducks are riding, in such a way that her face is hidden; the other does not show the wild girl, but shows the ducks battling her wild dogs (Scrooge is dressed in a protective suit, and four dogs are pulling at his arms and legs). Western's editors took longer than usual to decide on the cover for this issue, out of reluctance to show the wild girl (although this was eventually done), and even considered a straight gag cover of the kind used on most of the Dell issues. *(July 9, 1965, letter from Chase Craig to Barks)*

Reprinted: Uncle Scrooge No. 128, May 1976.

UNCLE SCROOGE The Queen of the Wild Dog Pack - 24 - Scrooge takes Donald and the nephews with him to his sheep station in Australia, where his herds are being raided by wild dogs that make hash of his fences and traps. The nephews discover that the dogs' leader is a wild girl, and the ducks set a trap for the girl, using the radio songs of Tweedy Teentwirp, a pop singer, as the bait. *(May 12, 1965)*

Reprinted: Uncle Scrooge No. 128, May 1976.
