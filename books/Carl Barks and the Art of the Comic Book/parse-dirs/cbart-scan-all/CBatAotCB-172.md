In December 1964, a ship carrying thousands of sheep sank in the harbor at Kuwait. Because of the health hazard involved (the city of Kuwait pumped its drinking water from the harbor), it was necessary to raise the ship rapidly, and Karl Kroyer, a Danish inventor, did so by filling the ship with expandable polystyrene foam. He sought a patent on the process, but, under patent law, a "preliminary description" had already been published, and, therefore, Kroyer's process was unpatentable. That "preliminary description" was this story. Accounts of this incident appeared in *Popular Science* for April 1965, and *Chemistry* for September 1966, both of which evidently obtained their information (which differs in some details) from Danish publications.
