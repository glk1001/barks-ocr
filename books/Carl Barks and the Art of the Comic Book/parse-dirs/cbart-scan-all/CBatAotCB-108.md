*Changes:* The dialogue in the second, third, fourth and fifth panels of the last page of this story was changed by Barks's editors. Barks does not recall the original dialogue.

*Weston:* In a letter to a fan dated March 22, 1961, Barks mentions "The Golden Christmas Tree": I felt sourly about the finished story because the editors had made me do some changes in the fight sequences between Don and the witch that I thought took the guts out of the story. I still gag when I read the last two pages of the story. But the rest of the tale was robust enough." In a more recent letter (October 3, 1973), Barks says, "About the last two pages of 'The Golden Christmas Tree'... only the last four panels are mine as they were in the original. I still wince at the preachy stuff I had to put in about the 'sprit of Christmas'." In earlier sections, the story seems to be intact up to page 9, but the bottom half of page 10 (probably redrawn) appears to have been drawn separately from the top half and a number of top and bottom halves from that point on don't quite match, so there were probably a couple of cuts or redrawn halves between
