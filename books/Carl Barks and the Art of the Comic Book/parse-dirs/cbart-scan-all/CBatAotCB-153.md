# 63 - May 1966 - 36 pages
Front cover: A frightened-looking Scrooge runs along, glowing from hat to spats, as the Beagle Boys head for a castle in the background. (Illustrating "House of Haunts.") *(Sept. 12, 1965)*
Reprinted: Uncle Scrooge No. 129, June 1976.
UNCLE SCROOGE House of Haunts - 24 - Scrooge transfers his money to a secret hiding place in anticipation of the release of the "rehabilitated" Beagle Boys from prison. The Beagle Boys spray him with a glowing paint so that they can follow him to his fortune, which is hidden in the "dreaded castle of the ancient mad duke of Duckburg." *(Aug. 3, 1965)*
Barks's title for this story was "His Shining Hour"; the title was changed by his editors.
Reprinted: Uncle Scrooge No. 129, June 1976.
# 64 - July 1966 - 36 pages
Front cover: Scrooge, dressed in oriental splendor, rides in a sedan chair borne by Donald and the nephews. (Illustrating "Treasure of Marco Polo.") *(Dec. 23, 1965)*
Reprinted: Uncle Scrooge No. 134, November 1976.
UNCLE SCROOGE Treasure of Marco Polo - 24 - Scrooge has bought a life-size jade elephant that has been shipped in a crate from the war-torn kingdom of Unsteadystan, but only the elephant's tail is in the trunk, along with a peasant boy named Soy Bheen. A note from Marco Polo in the elephant's tail tells of a treasure map in the elephant's trunk, and the ducks and Soy Bheen go to Unsteadystan, where the elephant is being used by the rebel leader, Wahn Beeg Rhat, as a sort of totem. *(Oct. 13, 1965)*
Reprinted: Uncle Scrooge No. 134, November 1976.

UNCLE SCROOGE The Heedless Horseman - 24 - Scrooge, determined to win the Crystal Orb Derby and become "king" of Duckburg for the coming year, buys Fireball, the horse that has won the two previous years —

# 68 - March 1967 - 36 pages
Front cover: The ducks swim into an underwater cave where a duck mermaid sits with a shark at her side. (Illustrating "Hall of the Mermaid Queen.") *(July 20, 1966)*
Reprinted: Uncle Scrooge No. 125, January 1976.
UNCLE SCROOGE Hall of the Mermaid Queen - 24 - The floor of Scrooge's money bin breaks through and most of his money slides through a cavern and out to sea, coming to rest many fathoms beneath the surface. Donald and Scrooge go after it in special diving gear, and on the ocean floor they encounter a strange race of duck mermen who are ruled by a mermaid queen. Daisy, fearing that Donald will be seduced, follows him to the bottom. *(Apr. 13, 1966)*
Reprinted: Uncle Scrooge No. 125, March 1976.
# 69 - May 1967 - 36 pages
Front cover: Scrooge is suspended in a sling from a helicopter, and he has just put his "$" brand on a longhorn steer. (Illustrating "The Cattle King.") *(Sept. 30, 1966)*
Reprinted: Uncle Scrooge No. 126, March 1976.
UNCLE SCROOGE The Cattle King - 24 - Scrooge brings Donald and the nephews to his ranch at roundup time so that he can teach them the cattle business. Scrooge's cowboys are fleeing from Snake McViper's rustlers, so the ducks take over their chores. McViper's men get the best of them — and get Scrooge's cattle — until two of the nephews disguise themselves as a girl and trick McViper's men into eating their abominable cooking. *(May 27, 1966)*
Reprinted: Uncle Scrooge No. 126, March 1976.
